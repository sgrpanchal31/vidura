Improving Language Models by Retrieving from Trillions of Tokens (RETRO)
Borgeaud, Mensch, Hoffmann, Cai, Rutherford, Millican, van den Driessche, Lespiau, Damoc, Clark, de Las Casas, Guy, Casanova, Graham, Brock, Paganini, Beaumont, Ziegler, Larom, Noy, Bamler, Wierstra, Vinyals, Osindero, Simonyan, Kavukcuoglu, Rae, Grefenstette, Sifre (2022)

We introduce RETRO (Retrieval-Enhanced Transformer), which retrieves from a database of 2 trillion tokens during training and inference. RETRO obtains comparable performance to GPT-3 and Jurassic-1 on the Pile, despite using 25× fewer parameters.

Architecture: RETRO is a decoder-only Transformer that retrieves chunks of text from a large external database at every attention layer. For each input chunk of 64 tokens, the model retrieves the k=2 nearest neighbor chunks from the database using approximate nearest-neighbor search (SCaNN). Retrieved neighbors are encoded with a frozen BERT-style encoder and integrated via cross-attention inside each Transformer layer.

Database: 2 trillion tokens from MassiveText. Pre-encoded with the retrieval encoder and stored for approximate nearest-neighbor lookup at training and inference time.

Model sizes evaluated: 172M, 425M, 1.5B, 7.5B parameters.

Key result: RETRO-7.5B achieves comparable perplexity to GPT-3-175B on C4 and Wikitext103 benchmarks, despite having 25× fewer parameters.

On knowledge-intensive question answering:
- RETRO improves upon a standard transformer baseline by a large margin on Natural Questions and TriviaQA
- Retrieval helps most on questions requiring rare or recent world knowledge

RETRO demonstrates that scaling the retrieval database is a more efficient path than scaling model parameters for many knowledge-intensive tasks.
