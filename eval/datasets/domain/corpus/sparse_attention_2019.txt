Generating Long Sequences with Sparse Transformers
Child, Gray, Radford, Sutskever (2019)

Transformers are powerful sequence models, but require time and memory that grows quadratically with the sequence length. We introduce sparse factorizations of the attention matrix that reduce this to O(n√n), while still allowing the model to attend to all positions within a window. We also introduce a recomputation scheme that reduces memory usage from O(n²) to O(n) while increasing computation by only a constant factor.

Two sparse attention patterns are introduced:
1. Strided attention: attend to local neighbors plus every stride-th position
2. Fixed attention: a subset of tokens attend to all previous tokens in the sequence

Sparse Transformers model sequences 30x longer than those typically modeled with dense attention, enabling modeling of sequences up to 12,288 tokens on a single GPU.

Results:
- CIFAR-10 image generation: 2.80 bits/dim (new state-of-the-art at the time)
- Enwiki8 text: 0.99 bits/char
- ImageNet 64×64: 3.44 bits/dim
- Classical music audio (raw bytes): state-of-the-art in audio generation

The sparse attention patterns achieve near-identical performance to full attention on standard benchmarks while using a fraction of the compute. Sparse Transformers demonstrated that long-range dependencies in images, text, and audio can be captured efficiently without quadratic attention.
