PaLM: Scaling Language Modeling with Pathways
Chowdhery, Narang, Devlin, Bosma, Mishra, Roberts, Barham, Chung, Sutton, Gehrmann, Schuh, Shi, Tsvyashchenko, Maynez, Rao, Barnes, Tay, Shazeer, Prabhakaran, Reif, Du, Hutchinson, Pope, Bradbury, Austin, Isard, Gur-Ari, Yin, Duke, Levskaya, Ghemawat, Dev, Michalewski, Garcia, Misra, Robinson, Fedus, Zhou, Ippolito, Lester, Longpre, Fiedel, Narayanan, Wei, Bosma, Hu, Soricut, Stern, Zoph, Chi, Dean (2022)

We introduce PaLM (Pathways Language Model), a 540 billion parameter autoregressive language model, trained using a new ML system called Pathways. Pathways enables highly efficient training across 6144 TPU v4 chips using two types of parallelism simultaneously.

PaLM uses the standard decoder-only Transformer architecture with modifications: SwiGLU activation, parallel formulation of attention and feed-forward layers, multi-query attention, RoPE embeddings, and vocabulary of 256K tokens using SentencePiece.

Training data: 780 billion tokens from social media conversations, filtered webpages, books, GitHub, multilingual Wikipedia, and news articles.

Results on 150 NLP benchmarks:
- Few-shot performance: PaLM 540B outperforms state-of-the-art fine-tuned models on 28 of 29 natural language understanding tasks
- BIG-Bench (hard reasoning tasks): PaLM 540B outperforms average human performance on 58% of tasks
- Chain-of-thought reasoning (GSM8K): 56.5% with 8-shot chain-of-thought prompting

Discontinuous improvements (abilities that emerge at PaLM 540B but not smaller PaLM sizes):
- Multi-step arithmetic (jump from 17% at 62B to 58% at 540B)
- Code translation between programming languages

PaLM achieves state-of-the-art on multilingual NLP and code benchmarks, demonstrating continued scaling benefits at the 500B+ parameter range.
