Generative Adversarial Networks
Goodfellow, Pouget-Abadie, Mirza, Xu, Warde-Farley, Ozair, Courville, Bengio (2014)

We propose a new framework for estimating generative models via an adversarial process, in which we simultaneously train two models: a generative model G that captures the data distribution, and a discriminative model D that estimates the probability that a sample came from the training data rather than G.

The training procedure is a minimax two-player game. G tries to fool D by generating realistic samples; D tries to distinguish real data from generated data. At the global optimum, G reproduces the training data distribution and D outputs 1/2 everywhere (cannot distinguish real from fake).

The loss function is:
min_G max_D E[log D(x)] + E[log(1 - D(G(z)))]

where x is real data and z is noise input to the generator.

Key properties:
- No Markov chains required; training uses only backpropagation
- No inference required during training
- The framework is general and applies to any differentiable generative model

Experimental results on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and Toronto Face Database demonstrate that GANs generate visually plausible samples. Qualitative comparisons with restricted Boltzmann machines and deep Boltzmann machines show competitive or superior sample quality.

GANs established a new paradigm for generative modeling that led to a decade of research on image synthesis, style transfer, data augmentation, and eventually diffusion models.
