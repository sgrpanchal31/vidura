Alpaca: A Strong, Replicable Instruction-Following Model
Taori, Gulrajani, Zhang, Dubois, Li, Guestrin, Liang, Hashimoto (2023)

We present Alpaca, an instruction-following model fine-tuned from LLaMA-7B on 52K instruction-following demonstrations generated by OpenAI's text-davinci-003. The total cost of fine-tuning is under $100, making instruction-tuning accessible to academic researchers.

Training data: 52,252 instruction-following demonstrations generated using the self-instruct method. Starting from 175 seed tasks, text-davinci-003 was prompted to generate new tasks and demonstrations. Deduplication and quality filtering removed similar or low-quality examples.

Fine-tuning: Standard supervised fine-tuning on LLaMA-7B using the generated instruction-output pairs. Training used 4 A100 80GB GPUs for 3 hours ($36 compute cost). Data generation cost approximately $500 using the OpenAI API.

Results: Alpaca performs comparably to text-davinci-003 (GPT-3.5) on the self-instruct evaluation benchmark. Human evaluators preferred Alpaca over text-davinci-003 on 90 of 252 tested instructions (36% win rate), compared to text-davinci-003 preferring itself on 114 instructions.

Alpaca exhibits many of the same instruction-following behaviors as ChatGPT, including:
- Following open-ended instructions
- Completing partially-written text
- Answering factual questions
- Writing stories and explanations

Alpaca demonstrated that distillation from large proprietary models (using their outputs as training data) can efficiently create capable instruction-following models at minimal cost, spawning a family of similar approaches (Vicuna, WizardLM, etc.).
