Tree of Thoughts: Deliberate Problem Solving with Large Language Models
Yao, Yu, Zhao, Shafran, Griffiths, Cao, Narasimhan (2023)

Language models are increasingly used for general problem solving. Current approaches (standard prompting, chain-of-thought) treat LMs as producing a left-to-right sequence of tokens in one pass. We propose Tree of Thoughts (ToT), a framework that generalizes chain-of-thought by allowing LMs to explore multiple reasoning paths and evaluate progress toward a goal — more like human deliberate reasoning.

Tree of Thoughts framework:
1. Decompose: Break a problem into thought steps — intermediate reasoning stages
2. Generate: At each step, generate k candidate thoughts (using the LM itself)
3. Evaluate: Use the LM to assess the promise of each partial solution (score/rank)
4. Search: Use breadth-first search (BFS) or depth-first search (DFS) to explore the tree of possible reasoning paths
5. Backtrack: Abandon unpromising branches and explore alternatives

Three test tasks:

Game of 24 (find arithmetic expression equaling 24 from 4 numbers):
- Standard prompting (GPT-4): 4% success rate
- Chain-of-thought (GPT-4): 4% success rate
- Tree of Thoughts (GPT-4, BFS b=5): 74% success rate

Creative Writing (write a coherent short story with 4 given sentences):
- Chain-of-thought: 6.93 human rating (1-10)
- Tree of Thoughts: 7.56 human rating (+9% improvement)

Mini Crosswords (5×5):
- Chain-of-thought: 16% of words correct
- Tree of Thoughts: 60% of words correct

ToT enables planning, lookahead, and backtracking — cognitive abilities that flat sequential generation cannot support. The LM both generates candidates and evaluates them, serving as both a generator and a verifier of its own reasoning.
