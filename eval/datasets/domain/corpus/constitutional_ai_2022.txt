Constitutional AI: Harmlessness from AI Feedback (CAI)
Bai, Jones, Ndousse, Askell, Chen, DasSarma, Drain, Fort, Ganguli, Henighan, Johnston, Kadavath, Kernion, Conerly, El-Showk, Elhage, Hatfield-Dodds, Hernandez, Hume, Kaplan, Khatri, Lasenby, Lightman, Lin, Mather, Matz, McCandlish, McKinnon, McMillan, Mirhoseini, Misra, Mitchell, Murati, Nanda, Olsson, Amodei, Mann, Perez, Pierson, Ryder, Shan, Sohl-Dickstein, Stiennon, Telleen-Lawton, Tenenbaum, Thacker, Tibshiram, Thomas, Torrance, Train, West, Ziegler, Zou, Kaplan (2022)

Constitutional AI (CAI) is a method for training AI systems to be harmless using a set of principles (a "constitution") rather than human-provided harmful/harmless labels.

The two-phase training procedure:

Phase 1 — Supervised Learning from AI Feedback (SL-CAI):
1. Generate responses to potentially harmful prompts using an initial model
2. Ask the model to critique its own response according to constitutional principles
3. Ask the model to revise its response to be more harmless
4. Fine-tune on the revised responses

Phase 2 — Reinforcement Learning from AI Feedback (RLAIF):
1. Generate pairs of responses to harmful prompts
2. Ask the model to choose which response is more harmless given a constitutional principle
3. Use these AI-generated preference labels to train a preference model (Harmless Reward Model)
4. Use PPO to fine-tune the policy against this reward model

The constitution contains 16 principles derived from sources including the UN Declaration of Human Rights, asking the model to consider harms, deception, rights violations, and potential misuse.

Key finding: CAI allows scaling harmlessness training without human labels for harm. RLAIF produces a harmlessness reward model that performs comparably to one trained on human-generated harm labels. The resulting model (Claude v1 prototype) is both more helpful than a purely RLHF-trained model and less harmful.

CAI established AI feedback as a viable alternative to human feedback for safety training, enabling more scalable alignment approaches.
